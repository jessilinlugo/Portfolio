TRACKSTAR is my 2nd collaborative project I recently helped design and complete. TRACKSTAR is an application in which a registered user can input a tracking number for their pending delivery, and track their package in "real time" as the arrows at the bottom update from Placed to In Transit. Once an item has been delivered, the package is moved to the delivered tab. I had a lot of fun creating the wire frame for this application, and the overall look and design of the website was a collaborative effort (except for the areas where I made Executive Artistic Decisions, such as the font used in the logo and headers). I call wire frames instructional manuals, because once you've created and are happy with your wire frame, the only thing left to do is follow the pictures and set up the code to match. I had a lot of fun designing the logo, icon, and interface, and it definitely cemented the idea that I'd like to go into the UI/UX field.

Will It Stream is a collaborative project I recently helped complete. Once a user has inputted a tv or movie title, two separate APIs (OMDb and uTelly) are called to display the relevant information. A poster, title, critics score, release date, runtime, and a brief description are all displayed as a result of the search. As well as any applicable streaming service icons leading directly to the title on the corresponding streaming platform. The User Interface is designed to mimic the cinema experience. With so many theaters shutting down, and our bedrooms and living rooms doubling as home theaters, Will It Stream was created for the home viewer in mind.

Week Day Scheduler was an exercise in JavaScript in which I really shined. The application is essentially a daily planner, in which to lay out and schedule your day. The planner runs on a timer and will change the color of the text box based on the time of day. The hours that have passed will be highlighted a light yellow, the current hour is a muted red, and the remaining hours of the day are a soothing green. I didn't want the color palette to be overwhelming for users, and felt the muted winter's day aesthetic was perfect for the short days and long nights.

Team Info Generator was the first Node/Javascript application I created that actually works. I spent a lot of time studying and reviewing class lectures in order to really understand this assignment. This application runs the terminal in Javascript and allows a user to run through a series of prompts in order to generate an HTML website containing their team's information. Different titles have different information listed, such as Managers have office numbers, Engineers have their Github username/repository linked, and Interns have their educational institute listed. Along with the single alternating line, each title has it's own corresponding icon to represent the employee. Because I was more focused on the functionality of the application, versus the UI, I didn't spend much time updating the HTML to match the aesthetic of most of my projects.

Salud Cafe began as a branding assignment, and eventually was developed into so much more. If you haven’t noticed already, I am Latina, and I love to use my culture for my designs. With Salud Cafe, I decided that it would be a restaurant with a specialty dish from each South American country. My dad was born in Mexico, and my mom was born in El Salvador, because of this, I’ve always been aware of the differences in each country’s culture and the nuance in which we prepare similar dishes. Each of these countries has a different way they prepare rice, or tamales, but each are delicious and amazing in their own respects. Everybody knows about fusion restaurants, but I wanted a restaurant that would prepare a signature dish for each South American country, with no substitutions for ingredients or cooking methods. I love my culture and where I’m from, and I love sharing it with others to learn and partake in as well. Food is a big thing in Latin American culture, it brings people together, and helps to smooth over arguments and when the food tastes good, you feel good.
