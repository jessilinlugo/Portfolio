My Collection of Works

Pretty Lugo Things is a personal brand I created where I can freely express whatever creative inspiration strikes me at any odd moment. I like to dabble in different art styles and I felt branching out on my own was the only way I was going to get to experiment with free reign. Also, I feel this helps give people a better idea of how versatile I can be, and can adapt to any design or theme presented to me. I like to do research to make sure I get even tiny details correct, but also just because I love learning new things and getting lost in new information. Additionally, I like to dabble in art forms that aren’t digital, in which I can flex my photography and photo editing skills but also show off my latest amazing new creation.

FUNK Magazine began as a college assignment, but it helped me discover my passion for magazine design. I love collecting magazines with interesting covers or titles. I love unusual and striking magazine layouts, and they tend to have thought provoking content to go along with it. Most of the copy in my magazine is filler because I’m just trying to showcase my layouts, but in another life I went into some writing career.

Salud Cafe began as a branding assignment, and eventually was developed into so much more. If you haven’t noticed already, I am Latina, and I love to use my culture for my designs. With Salud Cafe, I decided that it would be a restaurant with a specialty dish from each South American country. My dad was born in Mexico, and my mom was born in El Salvador, because of this, I’ve always been aware of the differences in each country’s culture and the nuance in which we prepare similar dishes. Each of these countries has a different way they prepare rice, or tamales, but each are delicious and amazing in their own respects. Everybody knows about fusion restaurants, but I wanted a restaurant that would prepare a signature dish for each South American country, with no substitutions for ingredients or cooking methods. I love my culture and where I’m from, and I love sharing it with others to learn and partake in as well. Food is a big thing in Latin American culture, it brings people together, and helps to smooth over arguments and when the food tastes good, you feel good.
